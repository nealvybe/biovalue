# Sourcebook for 11 Biopharma Deals

This document compiles public references that explain the economics, lead assets, stage at signing, and strategic rationale for 11 biopharma deals that fit the valuation workstream for BioValue.[web:94][page:1] The source set relies first on company disclosures and then on market reviews that place each transaction in context, especially where a deal involved more than one asset or a broader platform.[web:179][web:578][web:83]

## Scope

The 11 deals covered here are: Johnson & Johnson / Intra-Cellular Therapies, Merck KGaA / SpringWorks Therapeutics, Novartis / Anthos Therapeutics, Sanofi / Blueprint Medicines, Sobi / Arthrosi Therapeutics, AbbVie / Capstan Therapeutics, Eli Lilly / Verve Therapeutics, BioNTech / CureVac, Bain Capital / Mitsubishi Tanabe Pharma, Mallinckrodt / Endo, and Merck / EyeBio.[page:1][web:94][web:83] Some of these are clean single-asset or near-single-asset deals, while others are broader company acquisitions whose headline value reflects a portfolio or platform rather than one lead program.[page:1][web:578][web:1062]

## 1. Johnson & Johnson / Intra-Cellular Therapies

DealForma's Q1 2025 review states that Johnson & Johnson acquired Intra-Cellular Therapies in January 2025 for $14.6 billion in cash, equal to $132 per share and described as a 40% premium.[page:1] The same source explains that the strategic rationale was to strengthen J&J's position in CNS and neuropsychiatric disorders, led by commercial product Caplyta plus a broader late-stage and early-stage pipeline.[page:1]

Key references:
- DealForma Q1 2025 review: headline value, per-share value, premium, therapeutic focus, and summary of commercial and pipeline assets.[page:1]
- Fierce Pharma top M&A ranking: useful secondary cross-check that J&J opened 2025 with a large CNS-focused acquisition and that the transaction ranked among the year's biggest deals.[web:87]

Why it matters for modeling: this is not a single-asset acquisition, so using it as a direct rNPV comp for one drug would require isolating how much value belonged to Caplyta versus the rest of the pipeline.[page:1]

## 2. Merck KGaA / SpringWorks Therapeutics

DealForma states that Merck KGaA announced the SpringWorks acquisition in February 2025 for $3.5 billion in cash at $47 per share.[page:1] The review describes the company as focused on rare tumors and oncology, with approved product Ogsiveo and additional pipeline assets including mirdametinib and brimarafenib, indicating this was not a pure single-asset purchase.[page:1]

Key references:
- DealForma Q1 2025 review: official economics used in the market review, lead asset list, and stage context for Ogsiveo and other programs.[page:1]
- Merck KGaA announcement: buyer framing that the acquisition was intended to build a rare-tumor position and expand Merck KGaA's U.S. oncology footprint.[web:1062]
- DealForma large-cap M&A review: confirms the $3.5 billion value and positions Ogsiveo as the lead strategic asset in retrospective market context.[web:83]

Why it matters for modeling: SpringWorks can be used as a late-stage oncology/rare-tumor comp, but the presence of an approved product plus multiple additional assets means the headline acquisition price should not be assigned to one molecule without adjustment.[page:1][web:83]

## 3. Novartis / Anthos Therapeutics

Novartis announced in February 2025 that it would acquire Anthos Therapeutics for $925 million upfront and up to $2.15 billion in additional regulatory and sales milestones.[web:179] Anthos separately confirmed that the total potential value of the transaction was up to $3.1 billion and tied the transaction directly to abelacimab, a Factor XI inhibitor in Phase III and Phase II studies.[web:254][page:1]

Key references:
- Novartis press release: official economics and strategic rationale, including the $925 million upfront and up to $2.15 billion in milestones.[web:179]
- Anthos signing release: seller-side confirmation of the same economics and framing of abelacimab as the central asset.[web:254]
- Anthos closing release: confirms deal completion and reiterates total potential consideration up to $3.1 billion.[web:190]
- DealForma Q1 2025 review: stage context, including Phase III use in atrial fibrillation and venous thromboembolism and Phase II use in stroke and thrombosis.[page:1]
- BioPharma Dive and Fierce Biotech: independent reporting that Anthos was effectively a startup built around abelacimab and that the transaction returned the asset to Novartis.[web:184][web:137]

Why it matters for modeling: Anthos is one of the strongest public comps for a near-single-asset, late-stage cardiovascular acquisition with a heavily milestone-weighted structure.[web:179][page:1]

## 4. Sanofi / Blueprint Medicines

Sanofi announced in June 2025 that it would acquire Blueprint Medicines for about $9.1 billion upfront, with contingent value rights tied to BLU-808 milestones bringing total fully diluted value to about $9.5 billion.[web:578] The buyer and secondary market sources make clear that the deal included commercial product Ayvakit plus clinical assets elenestinib and BLU-808, so the transaction reflects a commercial-stage franchise and pipeline package rather than a single-asset purchase.[web:578][web:1057]

Key references:
- Sanofi announcement: official per-share consideration, CVR structure, premium paid, and total fully diluted value.[web:578]
- Sanofi closing release: confirms close and restates the strategic focus on rare immunology and mast-cell disease assets.[web:503]
- DealForma Q2 2025 review: confirms the market shorthand of $9.1 billion upfront plus up to $400 million in milestones and describes the major acquired assets.[web:1057]
- Labiotech June 2025 roundup: adds journalistic context on Blueprint's systemic mastocytosis positioning and Ayvakit as the first approved medicine in its disease area.[web:1059]
- BioSpace coverage: adds revenue and growth framing that helps explain why Sanofi paid a large upfront for a commercial product plus pipeline package.[web:1064]

Why it matters for modeling: Blueprint is useful as a high-end benchmark for commercial and near-commercial rare-disease/oncology value, but not as a clean one-asset comp.[web:578][web:1057]

## 5. Sobi / Arthrosi Therapeutics

BioSpace reported in December 2025 that Sobi agreed to acquire Arthrosi Therapeutics for $950 million upfront and up to $550 million in clinical, regulatory, and commercial milestones.[web:621] Fierce Biotech described Arthrosi as a gout specialist and confirmed that the transaction centered on pozdeutinurad, while H7 BioCapital stated that the asset was in two fully enrolled global Phase III studies at signing.[web:216][web:560]

Key references:
- BioSpace: full headline economics and asset description for pozdeutinurad.[web:621]
- Fierce Biotech: confirmation of the same deal terms and buyer framing around a late-stage gout asset.[web:216]
- H7 BioCapital announcement: confirms that both Phase III studies were fully enrolled and that data were expected in 2026.[web:560]
- Los Angeles Times analysis: adds strategic context around Sobi's gout franchise and why the company may have paid above a pure base-case rNPV.[web:494]

Why it matters for modeling: Arthrosi is one of the cleanest examples in the set of a late-stage, largely single-asset company where the acquisition price can be compared directly with an rNPV built from epidemiology, PoS, and market assumptions.[web:621][web:216]

## 6. AbbVie / Capstan Therapeutics

J.P. Morgan's Q2 2025 report identifies AbbVie / Capstan as one of the notable Q2 2025 biopharma acquisitions and lists the transaction among the quarter's headline deals.[page:1] Xtalks reports that AbbVie announced a deal worth up to $2.1 billion in cash, obtaining Capstan's in-vivo targeted lipid nanoparticle platform and lead asset CPTX2309, described as a Phase I in-vivo CD19 CAR-T for B-cell autoimmune diseases.[web:81]

Key references:
- J.P. Morgan Q2 2025 report: identifies the deal as one of the quarter's notable acquisitions and places it in the broader M&A context.[web:94]
- Xtalks roundup: provides accessible public detail on the transaction value, lead asset CPTX2309, platform scope, and completion date.[web:81]
- DealForma Q2 2025 review: confirms the $2.1 billion acquisition value and frames the strategic rationale as strengthening AbbVie's cell and gene therapy position through Capstan's in-vivo CAR-T and RNA delivery capabilities.[web:1057]

Why it matters for modeling: Capstan is not a pure single-asset deal because the value clearly includes both a platform and lead Phase I program, so any asset-level model would need a platform discount or allocation step.[web:81][web:1057]

## 7. Eli Lilly / Verve Therapeutics

Labiotech reported that Eli Lilly acquired Verve Therapeutics for $1.3 billion in June 2025, centered on base-editing assets for cardiovascular disease.[web:1059] Xtalks adds that the upfront value was about $1.0 billion, with total value up to $1.3 billion if milestones are reached, and identifies VERVE-102, a PCSK9-targeting gene-editing program, as the central program.[web:81]

Key references:
- Xtalks roundup: deal structure, including roughly $1.0 billion upfront and up to $1.3 billion total, plus description of VERVE-102 and cardiovascular focus.[web:81]
- Labiotech June 2025 roundup: confirms headline transaction value and strategic focus on gene-editing assets for rare or genetically defined cardiovascular disease.[web:1059]
- J.P. Morgan Q2 2025 report: provides market context showing that Q2 2025 M&A favored high-impact platform and therapeutic acquisitions even below the megadeal level.[web:94]

Why it matters for modeling: Verve sits between a single-asset and platform transaction because the lead program drove the deal narrative, but the value also reflected Verve's broader base-editing capabilities.[web:81][web:1059]

## 8. BioNTech / CureVac

J.P. Morgan's Q2 2025 report names BioNTech / CureVac as one of the notable Q2 2025 acquisitions.[web:94] Labiotech reports that BioNTech took over CureVac for about $1.25 billion, describing the target as an mRNA vaccine and immunotherapy company with clinical and preclinical programs across cancer and infectious disease.[web:1059]

Key references:
- J.P. Morgan Q2 2025 report: confirms the transaction as one of the quarter's key announced M&A deals.[web:94]
- Labiotech June 2025 roundup: gives accessible public detail on the approximately $1.25 billion value and the fact that CureVac brought multiple clinical and preclinical mRNA assets.[web:1059]

Why it matters for modeling: CureVac is not a single-asset comp; its value reflects platform, IP, and multi-program optionality in mRNA, which makes it more useful as a strategic-platform benchmark than as a one-drug comparator.[web:1059]

## 9. Bain Capital / Mitsubishi Tanabe Pharma

DealForma's Q1 2025 review states that Bain Capital announced in February 2025 the acquisition of Mitsubishi Tanabe Pharma for about $3.3 billion upfront, or JPY 510 billion, with expected close in Q3 2025.[page:1] The same source lists multiple marketed and pipeline assets across NMOSD, oncology, diabetes, schizophrenia, Parkinson's disease, myasthenia gravis, and systemic sclerosis.[page:1]

Key references:
- DealForma Q1 2025 review: official market-review summary of transaction value, timing, and portfolio breadth.[page:1]
- J.P. Morgan Q2 2025 report: useful for broader context showing that Q1 2025 featured several large acquisitions outside the single-asset category.[web:94]

Why it matters for modeling: this is clearly a diversified company acquisition and should only be used as a market-cycle or strategic-buyout reference, not as a direct asset-value comp.[page:1]

## 10. Mallinckrodt / Endo

DealForma reports that Mallinckrodt announced in March 2025 a stock-and-cash acquisition of Endo with enterprise value of $6.7 billion.[page:1] The review describes Endo as contributing 27 approved branded drugs, 101 generics, and 40 sterile injectables in development, with a post-merger spinout planned for the generics and injectables businesses.[page:1]

Key references:
- DealForma Q1 2025 review: headline enterprise value, broad transaction structure, and detailed description of Endo's commercial portfolio breadth.[page:1]
- J.P. Morgan Q2 2025 report: contextual evidence that 2025 M&A included both focused biotech buys and larger diversified portfolio transactions.[web:94]

Why it matters for modeling: this is not appropriate as a single-drug valuation comp because the transaction reflects mature commercial assets, generics, and manufacturing considerations rather than one development-stage asset.[page:1]

## 11. Merck / EyeBio

DealForma's large-cap M&A review states that Merck acquired EyeBio in July 2024 for up to $3.0 billion and identifies Restoret, a retinal-disease program, as the central asset.[web:83] Although the transaction predates 2025, it belongs in many recent comp sets because it is a good example of a private biotech acquisition built around one lead ophthalmology candidate with milestone-heavy economics.[web:83]

Key references:
- DealForma large-cap M&A review: confirms total value up to $3.0 billion and identifies Restoret as the lead retinal-disease asset.[web:83]
- J.P. Morgan Q2 2025 report: useful as a cross-cycle benchmark on how late-stage and de-risked assets have continued to command large upfronts and headline values even when overall deal volume is selective.[web:94]

Why it matters for modeling: EyeBio is a helpful near-single-asset benchmark in ophthalmology, especially for understanding how optionality around one lead program plus follow-on indications can inflate total consideration versus pure base-case rNPV.[web:83][web:94]

## Cross-deal observations

The strongest candidates in this 11-deal set for direct asset-level rNPV comparison are Anthos, Arthrosi, and to a lesser extent EyeBio and Verve, because the public narrative and economics are clearly tied to one lead asset or a very narrow asset cluster.[web:179][web:621][web:83] By contrast, Blueprint, SpringWorks, Intra-Cellular, Mitsubishi Tanabe, Endo, and CureVac are broader portfolio or platform acquisitions and should be treated as strategic market references unless value can be allocated across programs.[web:578][page:1][web:1059]

J.P. Morgan's Q2 2025 report also shows why these late-stage deals matter in context: it reports that large-cap biopharma median upfront cash and equity for Phase III in-licensing reached $790 million in H1 2025, while Q2 2025 M&A included notable acquisitions such as Blueprint, Capstan, and CureVac.[web:94] That backdrop supports using milestone-weighted late-stage deals as the best anchors when calibrating BioValue outputs against real transactions.[web:94][web:83]
