## Executive Summary

The 2024–2026 pharma licensing and M&A market has been defined by five structural forces: (1) an acute IRA-driven urgency to backfill large-molecule LOEs with late-stage small molecule assets, (2) a widening premium for oral vs. IV/SC reformulations in established markets (gout, IBD, RA), (3) a surge in ADC deal activity following the HER2 and TROP2 proof points, (4) scarcity pricing for fully enrolled Phase III assets with clean safety profiles, and (5) a re-rating of rare disease assets following FDA's increased willingness to approve on surrogate endpoints.

Total disclosed biopharma M&A and licensing deal value in 2024 exceeded $320B, the highest since 2019, driven by mega-deals (AstraZeneca/Fusion ~$2.4B, BMS/Karuna ~$14B close) and a long tail of sub-$2B single-asset acquisitions.

## Valuation Benchmarks by Therapeutic Area

### Oncology (Solid Tumors)
- **Phase I rNPV range:** $40M–$180M (biomarker-selected) / $15M–$60M (unselected)
- **Phase II rNPV range:** $150M–$600M
- **Phase III rNPV range:** $400M–$2,000M+
- **Typical deal upfront at Ph III:** $800M–$4B
- **Key driver:** Biomarker selection premium is 2–3x unselected. IO combination assets trade at premium to monotherapy.
- **Notable 2024–2025 trend:** ADC deals dominate. Average ADC Phase II deal upfront: $1.8B (2024). Linker/payload platform deals command further premium.

### Hematology
- **Highest overall LoA of any TA: 23.9%**
- **Phase II rNPV range:** $200M–$800M
- **Phase III rNPV range:** $600M–$2,500M
- **Key driver:** Well-defined biomarker populations, surrogate endpoints (MRD, CR rate) widely accepted, accelerated approval pathway routinely used.
- **2025 trend:** MDS/AML MDM2 inhibitors and menin inhibitors repriced upward after ivosidenib/olutasidenib/revumenib approvals.

### Immunology / Inflammation (RA, PsA, IBD, SLE)
- **Phase II rNPV range:** $300M–$1,500M (IBD TL1A/IL-23) / $100M–$400M (RA/PsA)
- **Phase III rNPV range:** $500M–$3,000M
- **Key driver:** Merck/Prometheus ($10.8B for a Phase II TL1A) reset IBD valuations. Oral mechanisms (JAKi, S1P, integrin) trade at 30–50% premium to SC biologics.
- **Royalty range (licensing):** 10–18% net sales for late-stage assets.

### Cardiovascular
- **Phase III rNPV range:** $400M–$1,500M
- **Key driver:** IRA small-molecule preference; oral FXIa/FXIb mechanisms repriced post-abelacimab data. PCSK9 oral programs attracting significant interest.
- **2025 example:** Novartis/Anthos abelacimab — $925M upfront for a Ph III FXIa inhibitor with differentiated bleed safety profile.

### Rare Disease (Orphan)
- **Phase II rNPV range:** $150M–$600M (small molecule) / $300M–$1,500M (gene therapy)
- **Phase III rNPV range:** $400M–$2,000M
- **Key drivers:** (1) Orphan drug exclusivity (7 years US, 10 years EU), (2) higher pricing power ($100K–$500K/yr), (3) accelerated approval on surrogate endpoints, (4) smaller trials = lower R&D cost.
- **CGT (Cell & Gene Therapy):** Valuations highly bimodal. Platform deals (Pfizer/Seagen style) command $5B+. Single indication programs: $300M–$800M at Phase I/II given manufacturing and durability uncertainty.

### Rheumatology / Gout
- **Phase III rNPV range (oral specialty):** $250M–$1,050M (BioValue base/bull for pozdeutinurad)
- **Key driver:** Oral URAT1 class premium vs. IV pegloticase; lesinurad LOE creates white space; Sobi commercial infrastructure synergy.
- **Comp:** Ardea/AstraZeneca lesinurad acquired for ~$1.26B in 2012 (pre-approval, Ph III); inflation-adjusted ~$1.7B in 2025 dollars — consistent with pozdeutinurad pricing.

## The 5 Structural Trends Driving 2025–2026 Valuations

### 1. IRA-Driven Small Molecule Urgency
The Inflation Reduction Act's 9-year vs. 13-year negotiation timeline disadvantage for small molecules relative to biologics is reversing BD priorities. Large pharma is now actively hunting for small molecule assets with strong IP estates and novel mechanisms, accepting higher upfront prices to secure longer negotiation-free windows. **Effect on valuations: +15–25% premium for oral small molecules with novel targets vs. biosimilar-exposed biologics.**

### 2. Oral vs. IV/SC Premium
Across every TA where an oral option competes with an established injectable, the oral commands a 30–50% valuation premium. Examples: oral GLP-1 vs. semaglutide SC; MORF-057 oral integrin vs. vedolizumab IV; pozdeutinurad oral vs. pegloticase IV. The premium reflects patient preference, compliance, and market expansion potential. **Effect: Oral mechanism = 1.3–1.5x TA-standard rNPV.**

### 3. ADC Deal Surge
Post-Seagen (Pfizer $43B, 2023) and post-DESTINY-Breast (AstraZeneca/Daiichi Sankyo), ADC deal activity has been relentless. Average Phase II ADC upfront in 2024: $1.8B. New linker/payload combinations and novel targets (PTK7, CEACAM5, FRα) are priced at significant premium to standard chemo. **Warning: Market showing signs of congestion; undifferentiated ADCs (similar target, similar linker) facing valuation compression in 2025–2026.**

### 4. Enrolled Phase III Scarcity Premium
A fully enrolled Phase III with topline data expected within 12–18 months trades at a 30–50% premium to the same asset pre-enrollment. The scarcity driver is twofold: (1) acquirer locks in de-risked timeline; (2) competitive bidding intensifies as fewer such assets exist. **Effect: Enrolled Ph III = Base rNPV × 1.3–1.5x vs. Ph III entry.**

### 5. Strategic Fit / Franchise Synergies
Deals where the acquirer has an existing commercial infrastructure in the indication consistently close above rNPV. Sobi/Arthrosi (Krystexxa franchise), AbbVie/ImmunoGen (oncology buildout), Merck/Prometheus (immunology platform) all reflect this. The synergy premium is real: acquirer's marginal cost of commercialization is $0 vs. $100–200M for a new entrant. **Effect: Strategic acquirer pays 1.2–1.5x rNPV vs. financial acquirer.**
