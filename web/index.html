▸ Methodology & source set
The library aggregates US epidemiology from authoritative public sources, with one citation per indication. Source mix: CDC (NHANES, BRFSS, IBD/CKD/arthritis/asthma/SCD surveillance, National Diabetes Statistics Report) for chronic disease prevalence; SEER for cancer annual incidence; AHA Heart Disease & Stroke Statistics for cardiovascular; NIMH for psychiatric prevalence; disease-society foundations (Alzheimer's Association, Parkinson's Foundation, National MS Society, NPF, Lupus Foundation, PKD Foundation) for specific conditions; peer-reviewed literature (Younossi, Marras, Saha, Raghu, Lichtenstein, Friedman, Nordestgaard, Rachakonda) for prevalence and stratification; DelveInsight for select branded market reports (refractory gout, MASH). Pricing reflects approved-drug WAC benchmarks in the indication class. For decision-grade analysis, refine each value with the disease-specific epi report.

▸ Glossary — what each field means
Prevalence rate: share of the US population with the condition at any given time. For chronic diseases this is what accumulates; for acute conditions, use annual incidence.

Chronic / refractory: the share of prevalent patients with persistent/long-term disease (vs. transient or resolved). Set 100% if all patients are chronic; set lower (e.g. 13% for refractory gout) when only a subset of the prevalent pool is the addressable disease state.

Diagnosed & treated: the share of chronic patients who have received a diagnosis and are actively in treatment. Many conditions (obesity, MASH, mild depression) are massively under-diagnosed and under-treated — this is the bottleneck.

Addressable: the share of treated patients this specific drug can reach, given its label, mechanism, and competitive position. For an oral URAT1 inhibitor that competes with IV pegloticase + dotinurad: ~25% of treated refractory gout. For a best-in-class GLP-1 in obesity: ~70%.

WAC price (gross): US Wholesale Acquisition Cost — the manufacturer's published list price per patient per year. This is the headline price before any discounts.

GTN (Gross-to-Net) discount: the discount from WAC to net realized revenue after all rebates: payer/PBM rebates, 340B discounts, Medicaid rebates, patient assistance programs, and prompt-pay discounts. Typical ranges: specialty oncology 20–30%; immunology biologics 40–55%; primary care chronic drugs 50–70%; orphan with concentrated payers 10–20%. Net price = WAC × (1 − GTN%).

WW / US multiplier: total worldwide net revenue as a multiple of US net revenue. Industry standard is 1.5× for typical specialty (i.e., US is ~67% of WW); orphan drugs trend higher (1.6–2.0×) because international pricing concentrates in Europe/Japan; primary care can be lower (1.3–1.5×).

Whose cost of capital?
This is the acquirer's WACC — the rate they use to discount the asset's expected cash flows. Typical bands: large pharma 8–10%, mid-cap specialty 10–12%, small-cap / focused biotech 12–14%. The acquirer's higher BD-realistic hurdle (14%) is applied in the Deal Analysis tab's Commercial-adjusted lower bound, alongside peak-realization and commercial-PoS haircuts.
Royalty (% net sales if licensed)
Royalty is the rate the acquirer pays the licensor (acquiree / developer) as ongoing % of net sales in licensing-style deals. Set to 0% for full acquisitions (acquirer owns all revenue). For licensing deals (e.g. AbbVie/IGI ISB 2001 "tiered double-digit"), set to the agreed rate.

Asset rNPV is not affected by royalty — it always represents full standalone value. The royalty stream PV is added as a separate component to Deal PV on the Deal Analysis tab, representing the licensor's continuing economic interest.

How this is calculated

rNPV = Σ [ CFₜ × P(reach t) / (1 + r)ᵗ ]. R&D costs are probability-weighted by cumulative likelihood of reaching each stage, then discounted at WACC. Commercial revenue = addressable WW peak pool × peak market share × ramp × year-by-year discount × cumulative LoA, with LoE haircut on terminal years.

PoS uses a two-pass model: baseline from MIT Project ALPHA (database snapshot 2025-12-28, biomarker-stratified by therapeutic area where ALPHA covers the indication group), then marginal uplifts from differentiation flags compound multiplicatively on failure rate. ALPHA reports P(III→Approval) combined; BioValue splits it into P(III→NDA) × P(NDA→Approval) using a fixed 0.91 NDA-to-Approval rate. Per-transition cap: 90%. Price premium from flags compounds and caps at +50%. Commercial life additions cap at +5y. R&D cost penalties cap at +50%.

ALPHA snapshot files: data/mit_alpha/Main_20251228.txt and Biomarker_20251228.txt. TAs outside ALPHA's 15-category taxonomy (Rare Disease, Cell & Gene Therapy, Vaccines) use Wong-Siah-Lo / industry-aligned estimates flagged with the est. badge. Phase durations and R&D costs: Sertkaya et al. (JAMA Netw Open 2024); Chandra & Mazumdar (JOIM 2024). TA peak-rNPV ranges and deal comps: BioValue MARKET_REPORT.md. Royalty matrix: BioPharma Dealmakers 2024; Evaluate Pharma 2024.

How Deal PV is computed
Upfront is certain. Regulatory milestones are paid on approval — risk-adjusted by the model's cumulative LoA from current stage and discounted at WACC over time-to-launch. Sales milestones are paid ~4 years post-launch on hitting a sales threshold — risk-adjusted by LoA × sales-achievement probability and discounted from that later point.
Note on data: Public deal announcements typically disclose the total milestone pool but not the regulatory-vs-sales split or the sales-threshold size. Regulatory milestone share is an analyst estimate where not disclosed; sales-milestone achievement probability is always analyst convention (~70% industry standard). For deals like Farabursen (100% reg CVR) or VG-3927 (commercial CVR), the split is confirmed; for most others, it's estimated.
Royalty: set on the Capital tab. Use 0% for acquisitions (acquirer owns 100% of revenue). For licensing deals, enter the agreed rate (e.g. 14% for AbbVie/IGI's "tiered double-digit" on ISB 2001). Asset rNPV is unaffected — the royalty stream PV is added separately into Deal PV above.
Where the Deal PV sits in the [Commercial-adjusted, Asset rNPV] range.
How the Commercial-adjusted is computed
Re-run the rNPV engine with three fixed adjustments: WACC = 14% (acquirer's BD hurdle), peak × 0.60 (40% haircut for analyst forecast bias), launch probability × 0.85 (commercial PoS post-approval).
Two waterfalls: Asset rNPV → Commercial-adjusted (lower bound), and Upfront → +Reg PV → +Sales PV = Deal PV.
